Saniderm and similar breathable medical adhesive bandages stay on for 3-5 days. They trap plasma against the wound, prevent scabbing, and cut healing time roughly in half. Replace if seal breaks or pus accumulates.

curator: tattd-studio-dev

---

The dry-heal method skips moisturizing ointments. Wash twice daily with mild fragrance-free soap, pat dry. Suited to clients with sensitive skin who react to lanolin/petroleum products. Trade-off: tighter, more itchy healing experience than the moist-heal alternative.

curator: tattd-studio-dev

Moist-heal applies a thin layer of fragrance-free moisturizer (Aquaphor, Hustle Butter, A&D) 2-4 times per day after washing. Reduces itch and tightness compared to dry-heal. Apply thinly: thick layers suffocate the wound and slow healing.

Avoid direct sun on a fresh tattoo for the first 4-6 weeks. UV degrades both healing skin and ink saturation. After healing, daily SPF 30+ on visible tattoos extends color and detail life by years.

curator: tattd-studio-dev

No pools, oceans, hot tubs, or full-bath submersion for 2-3 weeks. Chlorine, salt water, and pool bacteria pose infection risk while skin is open. Showers (water running over the tattoo, not pressurized against it) are fine from day one.

Itching peaks around days 5-10 as scab/dry-skin layer forms. Do not scratch or pick scabs — pulling away healing skin pulls ink with it, leaving patchy spots. Tap or slap gently if itch is intolerable. Moisturizer reduces tightness-driven itch.

chunk_id: aftercare-infection-signs

title: Recognizing infection

curator: tattd-studio-dev
capture_date: 2026-05-06
synthetic: true
permission: synthetic-content-tattd-studio-poc

Normal: redness, swelling, mild warmth for 2-3 days. Concerning: pus discharge, fever, expanding redness past 1cm beyond the tattoo, red streaks toward heart, persistent warmth or pain after day 5. Seek medical care for any of these.

chunk_id: aftercare-allergic-reaction
area: aftercare
title: Allergic reactions to ink
source_url: https://www.tattd-studio.example/aftercare/allergic-reaction
curator: tattd-studio-dev
capture_date: 2026-05-06
synthetic: true
permission: synthetic-content-tattd-studio-poc
---

Red ink is the most common allergen (cinnabar/cadmium historically; modern reds are cleaner but still highest risk). Reactions can appear immediately or surface years later. Symptoms: localized itching, raised bumps, persistent swelling. Refer to a dermatologist; not all "infections" are bacterial.
