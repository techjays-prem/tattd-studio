Traditional Japanese irezumi has historic associations with the yakuza, and full-body suits remain socially fraught in Japan: many onsen (hot springs) and gyms refuse entry to the visibly tattooed. Discuss this with clients planning travel; cover-up sleeves are commonly used.

title: Tā moko (restricted) vs kirituhi (open)
source_url: https://www.tattd-studio.example/cultural/maori-tamoko-restriction
curator: tattd-studio-dev
capture_date: 2026-05-06
synthetic: true
permission: synthetic-content-tattd-studio-poc
---

Tā moko is a Maori practice that encodes whakapapa (genealogy); it's traditionally reserved for Maori. Non-Maori clients receive *kirituhi* (Maori-style tattoos without genealogical specificity). A studio should be explicit with non-Maori clients about which tradition they're being given.

Tattoos in non-English scripts have a long history of mistranslation: Chinese tattoos meaning "soup" instead of "love", Japanese characters that don't form a coherent word, mirrored Arabic. Require a native-speaker confirmation of the final exact wording in the destination script before applying.
